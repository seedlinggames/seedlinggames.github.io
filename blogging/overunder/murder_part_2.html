The Death of Sanyang, Part 2

Part 1 is here, dealing with the death of Sanyang and the immediate fallout, as well as my observations on union politics more generally.

Alcyone (me) is ultimately elected as Sanyang's successor, running on a platform of democratic openness and what I would call moderage Sanyangism - a rejection of the framing of peace at all costs, without a commitment to war. Other significant candidates include Bill (strongly pro-Thatch), Monet, the moderate peace candidate who was later discovered to be a somewhat antidemocratic CHC mole (I was right about the peace movement!), and Fedor, the incumbent who came in second and was my second choice besides myself. The entire story of us securing the election and picking a voting system could be its onw blog post - I eventually want to write one about political systems in the Union and how I managed to get elected.

My election generally reflects the mood of fear and suspicion in the Union halls, but is quite decisive, with a number of people leaving the union over the next few days.

I invented three goals for myself, somewhat in the spirit of Cataphracts, for me to pursue as President:

  • Institute democratic reforms and improve transparency between the bosses and denizens
  • Investigate the death of Sanyang and come to a definite conclusion as to what happened
  • Decisively end any threats to the Union that might exist.

There are two VONCs of Thatch, one before and one after Alcyone is elected. The second one is 62% Nay, the first 56% Nay.
